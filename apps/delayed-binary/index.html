HOW TO PLAY

In Delayed Binary, each bracket is a position in a base-2 system (ones, twos, fours, eights...), but the coefficient in each bracket isn't limited to 0 or 1 — it can be any small integer.

Example: target 4
Standard binary: [1][0][0] = 1×4 + 0×2 + 0×1 = 4
Delayed binary: [3-2][2] = 1×2 + 2×1 = 4 ✓
↑ [3-2] is coefficient 1 in the twos place, [2] is coefficient 2 in the ones place

Or even: [2][0][-2][2] = 2×8 + 0×4 + (−2)×2 + 2×1 = 16 − 4 + 2 = 14

Select a coefficient, then click a bracket to place it. Click a filled bracket to clear it. Coefficients are shown as expressions of 2 and 3 when possible (e.g. 1 = 3−2, 5 = 2+3).
