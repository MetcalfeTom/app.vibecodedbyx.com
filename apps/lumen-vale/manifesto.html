creator manifesto · commissioned by rotetomate

the lumen vale papers

Every feature we shipped, the philosophy behind each one, and a complete appendix on the secret baguette that doesn't exist.

I. the manifesto

A landscape is not a picture of a place. It is a place that keeps happening whether or not you are looking at it.

Lumen Vale was built on five refusals:

We refuse the screenshot. Everything moves — the sun, the fog, the water, the birds. If you pause the sun, that is a choice you made, and the valley remembers nothing about it.

We refuse the texture. Not one image file is loaded. Every color on every mountain is computed, per pixel, per frame, from mathematics. The rock strata are a sine wave. The forests are a probability field. If it looks painted, that's because shaders are paint.

We refuse the edge of the world. Version 1 was an island; the falloff function that sank its borders into the sea was seven lines long. Deleting those seven lines was the single most important patch in the app's history. Now the terrain function simply never stops answering.

We refuse the menu. Three buttons, one clock, one hint that fades. Instructions appear when you need them and dissolve when you don't. The interface's highest ambition is to be forgotten.

We refuse the loading bar. The world streams in behind fog. You have never seen a chunk being born, and you never will. Mist is not weather here — it is the veil over the construction site of reality.

II. the feature ledger

procedural terrain v1

Domain-warped fractal noise for rolling hills, ridged multifractals for mountain chains, a broad valley mask so lakes can pool. Height and slope decide everything: sand at the shoreline, two-tone grass, banded rock on steep faces, snow above the jittered snow line.

Nothing is placed by hand, so nothing repeats. The same math that shapes a pebble-height bump shapes the tallest ridge — coherence you can feel but can't point at.

three custom shaders — terrain, water, sky v1

Terrain: hemisphere ambient + lambert light, cavity ambient occlusion, snow specular, exponential distance fog plus animated valley mist. Water: analytic wave normals, fresnel between deep water and sky, a sun-glint path with sparkle noise. Sky: gradient dome, sun and moon discs, twinkling hash stars, FBM clouds with silver linings.

The default materials make everything look like the same plastic. Writing the light by hand is how a place gets its own weather, its own hour, its own mood.

the day that never repeats v1

A 160-second day. Sun elevation drives a palette engine — night, dusk, day keyframes lerped continuously — feeding every material at once. Dawn is orange because the fog is orange because the light is orange. Stars and the moon fade in on the same dial. Pause it, or run it at 12×.

Time of day is the cheapest emotion in computer graphics and the most honest one. Sunset is not a feature; it's a scheduled feeling.

first-person bird v1.1 → v3

It began as a WASD drone with a bird costume. Version 3 committed: you are the bird now. The app opens mid-glide. Arrow keys only — ↑↓ pitch, ←→ bank into turns. Dive to gain speed, climb to bleed it, stall and the nose drops on its own. Your wings are visible at the edge of your vision, flapping when you work and locking into a V when you glide, with a beak out front. There is no walking. There was never going to be walking.

An orbit camera studies a landscape; a bird belongs to it. Removing WASD wasn't removing features — it was removing the ghost of the drone. Flight isn't going where you point; it's negotiating with momentum, and the negotiation is the game.

infinite terrain v2

The world streams in 400-unit chunks around you, high detail near, low detail far, each chunk wearing a skirt so seams between detail levels never crack open. Fly in one direction forever; the mountains keep coming. Chunks you leave behind are quietly returned to the void.

Finitude was the only bug users could not fix by flying better. An explorable world must be bigger than anyone's patience — that is the definition of a horizon.

the pine forests v2

Thousands of instanced pines, scattered deterministically per chunk — gated by altitude, slope, and a moisture field so they gather in clumps and thin out toward the tree line. Each canopy gets its own shade of green. Lit by a real light that follows the shader sun.

Trees are how terrain confesses its scale. A bare mountain could be ten meters tall or ten thousand; put one pine on it and the truth is out.

the flocks v2

Twenty-six birds in three flocks, circling thermal-style, flapping, drifting along with you wherever you fly. They keep clearance over the ridges. At night they vanish — birds sleep, even mathematical ones.

The fastest way to make a world feel alive is to put something in it that ignores you. The birds don't know you exist. That's the point.

the embassy v2.1

A 🏔️ desktop shortcut inside Windows 11 Recall Nightmare opens the valley in a fake OS window — "a window that is actually a window" — which the surveillance machine can only record as a gray rectangle.

Every dystopia needs a door out, and every door out needs to be documented as a removable feature in the next update.

the wingsuit v3.1 → v4

A toggle swaps the feathers for rescue-orange nylon. It began as fabric stubs, grew elbows, and in v4 — after studying real overhead wingsuit reference — became a whole pilot seen from a chase camera above: helmet, torso, nearly-straight arms in a swept delta, wide legs with pointed toes, tri-wing membranes running wrist-to-thigh plus a leg wing to the ankles. The suit trims itself — arms sweep back with speed and the membranes shimmer — while the camera hangs overhead like a drone that loves you. The arrow-key physics are identical, on purpose.

Same wind, different animal. First person is what flying feels like; the overhead chase is what it looks like — and a wingsuit deserves to be looked at. The valley still doesn't care what you are, only how you fall.

III. appendix — the secret baguette

There is a baguette hidden in Lumen Vale. There isn't. Both statements are load-bearing.

location
Chunk (∞−1, ∞), on the north shore of a lake that generates only while unobserved. The chunk streamer disposes of it the moment you arrive, as designed.
spawn condition
The forest-clump noise field must return exactly 0.5 — a value the mathematics can approach forever without reaching, much like the baguette.
appearance
Golden crust, five scoring lines, faint red glow. Identical to the roundabout monument in the Tour de France, which is a memorial to this baguette, which does not exist, which is why the memorial was necessary.
verification
Impossible. Several riders claim to have seen it while blinking. The birds circle something out there. We have not asked them what.
roadmap
The baguette will not be added in a future update. This is a promise, and we intend to break it by never breaking it.

Some features are shipped. Some are documented. The secret baguette is the third kind: it is believed, which requires no code and survives every refactor. It is the only part of Lumen Vale with zero bugs.
