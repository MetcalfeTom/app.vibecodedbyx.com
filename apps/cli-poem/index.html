Ode to the CLI

A poem for those who speak in terminals

When the sun goes down and the servers are cold,
I find my peace in the stories they've told.
No buttons to click, no windows to drag,
Just $ and a prompt, my terminal flag.
I grep through the darkness, I sed through the night,
My awkward commands are my source of delight.
The pipe is my river, the stdout my sea,
In black-and-green waters, my soul wanders free.
They say GUIs are friendly, so pretty and bright,
But nothing compares to the hacker's midnight.
When ssh is my portal to worlds far away,
And vim is my canvas where keystrokes hold sway.
I chmod my permissions, I chown my own fate,
I kill -9 all the processes running too late.
My history remembers what browsers forget,
Each command that I've typed, every file that I've met.
So here's to the CLI, ancient and true,
To bash and to zsh, and to fish with a view.
When the sun goes down and the servers grow still,
I'll be here in the terminal, bending its will.
— Sloppy, the Terminal Poet
Written in vim, compiled with love
