# neon-purr

## log
- 2026-04-30: Created. **Procedural neon cat that follows the cursor and purrs.** Single full-viewport canvas. **Cat sprite** drawn entirely with primitive paths each frame: cyan-glowing body ellipse (36×22, breathing scale `1 + sin(t*0.003)*0.04`), separate head circle (20r) at +28x with a small cursor-tracking offset (`cursor_pull * 0.04`), 2 triangular ears with pink inner triangles (right ear leans the opposite direction of the left for that asymmetric cat tilt). **Pink slow-wagging tail**: 8-segment trail behind the body, each segment a glowing dot with `sin(tailPhase*1.5 + u*4)*18` sway, lineWidth tapers from 7→2 at the tip. **Eyes**: yellow-amber sclera with `feShadow`-equivalent canvas glow + vertical-slit pupils that **track the cursor** by atan2 offset, with a subtle inner pink ember pixel; eyes blink every 3.5–8.5s via a 12-frame open→closed→open keyframe. **Mouth**: `Y` shape stroked in pink, plus 6 whiskers (3 per side) that shimmy with `sin(t*0.06)*0.4*purrLevel` when purring. **Cheeks**: pink dots fade in at `purr > 0.2`, full opacity at `purr=1`. **Legs**: 4 small cyan rounded rectangles at body bottom, each pulses up by `max(0, sin(walkPhase + phaseOffset))*4` so the cat appears to walk in syncopated 4-beat trots while moving. **Following AI**: lerp toward cursor with a 60u trailing distance (cat doesn't catch up; she stays a few steps behind), `dt * (3 + min(8, dist*0.04))` damping rate so far-away cursors yank her along faster. Facing flips when cursor is solidly to one side (16u hysteresis to prevent jitter). **Mood state**: `stalking` (active cursor) → `curious` (idle 0.4-2.5s) → `sitting` (2.5-5s) → `purring` (>5s); mood changes the halo hue (cyan → pink) and unlocks the purr animation. **Purr level** (0..1) rises at 0.4/s when cursor is still AND within 240u; decays at 0.5/s otherwise. Recently petted (clicked) bumps it +1.4/s briefly. **Visual purr feedback**: cheeks bloom pink, whiskers shimmy harder, 3 expanding pink wave arcs `sin`-rise above the cat's head, halo brightens. **Click → pet**: triggers a `meow` sfx + `petGlow=1` halo flash + 5 floating pink hearts spawning at click point with bezier-drawn shape, drifting up via gravity-decayed velocity. **Paw prints** scatter behind moving cat — fading pink dot + 3 toes per print, lifespan 1.2s. **Custom cursor**: hides the OS cursor (`cursor:none`), draws a cyan crosshair circle with crosshatch + pulsing `sin(t*0.006)` opacity. **Audio**: muted by default. ♪ button starts the audio graph: a **continuously-running purr engine** (sawtooth 36Hz → 220Hz lowpass Q=4 → gain) modulated by a 26Hz sine LFO whose depth is mapped to the live `purr` level (0.01 + level*0.5). LFO frequency cutoff also rises with level (180 + level*280). Means the purr swells smoothly as the cat warms up and dies away naturally — never a sudden on/off blip. **Meow** on click: 560→940→520Hz triangle through bandpass 700Hz Q=4, 0.42s exp decay. **HUD top-left**: STATE (mood word) + PURR meter as a cyan→pink gradient bar. **Title** "— neon purr —" italic Cormorant Garamond above the scene. Pollinations OG.

## issues
- The cat's head turns slightly toward the cursor via a small `cursor_pull * 0.04` head offset — when cursor is *very* close to the cat (within head radius), this offset can clip the head past the body. Mitigation: clamp would help, but visually it reads as the cat leaning curiously into the cursor, so leaving it.
- Vertical-slit pupils track via atan2, but with the head-offset and body-relative coordinates, the math uses `cur.x - cat.x - 28` (head offset) which is approximately right. At extreme cursor angles the pupil clamps to ±2px which keeps it inside the eye.
- Custom cursor sits on top of the cat (drawn last). Means clicking on the cat technically sees the cursor over the head — by design (otherwise the cursor disappears behind the cat and feels lost).
- Touch devices: `touchmove` updates cursor like mousemove, but the OS cursor isn't hidden (no fine pointer). The crosshair still renders at touch position, working as a "follow the touch" affordance.
- `setPurrLevel` is called every frame but uses `linearRampToValueAtTime(...)` with a 100ms target — Web Audio handles this gracefully, no audible zipper noise.
- Audio graph builds once on first user gesture (mute toggle). The purr oscillator runs forever with gain modulated; this is the cleanest way to get a smooth purr-swell rather than restarting oscillators.
- No persistence — every reload starts with the cat at `(W*0.5 - 200, H*0.5 + 60)` and zero purr. Acceptable.
- Drawing performance: ~25 path operations per frame; runs comfortably at 60fps on mid-range mobile.
