# orbit-stack · notes

## log
- 2026-05-13: Shipped v1. Per chat: "3D spherical Tetris where pieces fly from all directions toward a central core." Built on **three.js r128** (CDN: cdnjs.cloudflare.com/ajax/libs/three.js/r128/three.min.js — same version used by rune-scroll). Full-viewport WebGL canvas with PerspectiveCamera at (0, 0.6, 6.5) looking at origin. Scene contains: **starfield** (900 white additive-blend Points scattered on radius 30-70 sphere, slowly rotating 0.012 rad/s on Y), **coreGroup** (the rotating container holding everything stack-bound — core mesh + grid lines + locked cubes + landing highlighter), and the **active piece + trail + bursts** which live in world space so they don't rotate with the core. **The core**: low-poly IcosahedronGeometry r=1.0 with cyan wireframe + faint dark-blue solid sphere just behind (creates the "glowing crystal" look) + inner white pulse sphere whose opacity sin-waves at 3Hz for ambient breathing. **Cell grid**: 6 latitude bands × 12 longitude slots × 5 layers max (so 360 cells total, plenty of room). Latitude rings + longitude meridians drawn as faint blue line strips at the core surface so the cell boundaries are visible against the cubes. **Mapping helpers**: `pointToCell(localVec3)` does `acos(y/r)` for latitude bin + `atan2(z,x)` for longitude bin; `cellToPos(lat, lon, layer)` returns the world-space center of the cell at the given layer (radius = 1.0 + cubeSize/2 + layer × cubeSize). **The pieces**: each new cube spawns at a **random direction on radius 7.5** (uniform on sphere via `acos(2r-1)` for theta + uniform phi), with velocity = `start.negate().normalize() × (1.7 to 2.2 u/s)`. So pieces visibly approach from ANY angle, fly straight at the core, rotate themselves while flying (x rate 2 rad/s, y rate 1.5 rad/s), pulse-scale ±8% at 6Hz to feel alive. A **white-additive trail** of 60 Points marks the recent flight path. Each piece has a randomized HSL hue (37° + random 40° step from the previous, so colors don't repeat). **Predicted-landing highlighter**: a wireframe gold cube that lives in coreGroup local space — each frame, the active piece's world position is inverse-transformed into coreGroup local, fed to `pointToCell()` to find target (lat, lon), checked against `topLayerAt()` to compute newLayer, then positioned at `cellToPos(lat, lon, newLayer)` so the player sees EXACTLY where the piece will land if the core isn't rotated further. Highlighter hides if newLayer would exceed MAX_LAYER (=5). **Lock**: when piece's distance-from-origin ≤ `layerCenter(newLayer) + cubeSize × 0.42`, lockPiece() removes the active mesh, builds a locked cube (slightly less glowy, white edge outlines opacity 0.5), positions it at the cell's local-space position, attaches to coreGroup (so it rotates with the stack), updates `stack` Map keyed `${lat}_${lon}_${layer}`, fires an 18-particle additive burst, plays a square-blip lock sound + sub thump, awards +10 base + 5×layer bonus, then schedules the next piece 350ms later. **Ring clear**: after each lock, sweep every (lat, layer) pair — if all 12 longitudes are filled at that combination, all 12 cubes dissolve into bursts, +100/ring + 50/extra-ring-cleared simultaneously, 5-note triangle arpeggio plays. **Game over**: piece would lock at layer ≥ 5 — that cell's stack overflowed → final burst, "Collapse" end overlay with score + rings cleared. **Rotation control**: pointer drag rotates coreGroup directly (dx/dy × 0.008 rad each pointermove); arrow keys / WASD apply angular acceleration (4 rad/s² accel, ±3.5 rad/s clamp, 0.92/frame damp for inertia); pitch clamped to ±1.2 rad so you can't tumble upside down. Space drops the piece immediately to its predicted cell (snaps to cellLocal position via coreGroup.localToWorld for the burst origin). **Audio** (Web Audio synth, mute toggle): whoosh on spawn (450ms band-pass-swept noise), lock (420→140Hz square + 110→40Hz sub thump), ring clear (5-note triangle arp G-C-E-G-C', up to 5 simultaneous rings), game over (1s lowpass-swept noise + 440→60Hz saw). **HUD**: 3 cells (Score gold / Rings magenta / Next preview with hue swatch), title strip centered up top in Audiowide cyan→bone→magenta gradient + Cormorant italic subtitle "rotate. *line them up.*", bottom button row with mute + restart + key-legend. **Overlays**: intro card explains the mechanic, end card shows final score + ring count. **Accessibility**: rem-based UI sizing, role=application on canvas with summary aria-label, role=status+aria-live on score, role=dialog+aria-modal on overlays with aria-hidden toggling, focus-visible gold outlines, ≥44px touch targets on all buttons, prefers-reduced-motion kills transitions (gameplay animations preserved since they ARE the game). Pollinations OG (seed 8800). Fonts: Audiowide + Rubik Mono One + Space Mono + Cormorant Garamond italic.

## issues
- Ring clears only check per-LAYER (all 12 lons at a given lat AND layer must be filled). A bottom layer can be full while top layers above stay — only the layer with the complete ring dissolves, no cascade. Could add "drop down" cascade (cubes above fall to fill the gap) but that complicates the spherical physics. v1 keeps it static.
- The lock detection uses scalar distance-from-origin against the predicted cell's layer height. Pieces arriving at high angle on already-stacked cells lock cleanly; pieces arriving on empty cells lock at layer 0 right at the core surface. Both look right in practice.
- coreGroup pitch is clamped at ±1.2 rad (≈ ±69°) to prevent the player from looking at the back of the sphere through the floor. Could expand to full ±π/2 (±90°) if chat wants. Yaw is unclamped (continuous rotation).
- The active piece's velocity is constant (no acceleration toward center), so flight time varies slightly with the random start direction's distance (7.5 ± small jitter from random). Average ~3 seconds for the player to react and rotate.
- MAX_LAYER = 5. With 12 lats × 6 lons × 5 layers = 360 cells, the player can lose pretty quickly if they don't strategize cell choice. Layer count tuning is a future tweak.
- Three.js r128 is old (the project standard) but works fine here; modern r150+ would need import-map setup.
- Drag rotation rate (0.008 rad/px) is calibrated for desktop mouse. Touch users might want it punchier — easy tweak.
