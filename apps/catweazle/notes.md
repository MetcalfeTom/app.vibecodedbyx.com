- 2026-05-16: v1 — whimsical text RPG. Chat ask (combined): "create a whimsical RPG called Catweazle set in rural England 2026, featuring an 11th-century wizard and his toad Touchwood" + "build the Catweazle RPG, a comedic text adventure where you use magic to battle elec-trickery." Both satisfied by the same build, which is consciously a love letter to the 1970 LWT show. **Premise**: Catweazle wakes in a strange barn with Touchwood on his chest. The barn is in 2026. His goal is to gather three runes (iron, glass, voice) and return to 1066 via the ancient standing stones at the edge of the village. Along the way he must not be broken by **bewilderment** (the meter that fills every time a modern object asserts itself). **Stats**: 3 meters — Hexes (0-5, currency for casting spells), Lore (0-50, wisdom; some choices gated by it later), Bewilderment (0-100, fail at 100 → he's sent to a "wellness retreat in Surrey" with Touchwood in a plastic tub named "Kenneth"). Stats render as parchment-bar gauges with rose/gold/moss fills; Bewilderment turns amber over 60 and rose-red over 85. Plus a Runes HUD (three medieval `I / II / III` discs) that light up gold as you collect. **Scenes**: 19 hand-authored cards (`s_start`, `s_lane`, `s_phone`, `s_phone_hex`, `s_car`, `s_car_hex`, `s_field`, `s_drone`, `s_pub`, `s_pub_eject`, `s_village`, `s_vicar_confess`, `s_tesco`, `s_tesco_alarm`, `s_stones`, `s_return`, `s_end_win`, `s_end_lose`, `s_no_hex`). Each scene has a title, a `prose()` function that renders multi-paragraph HTML (with `.touchwood` italic green-highlight callouts for the toad's commentary, `.modern` small-caps for objects he's mis-naming, and a CSS `::first-letter` drop cap on the opening paragraph), and a `choices[]` array. Each choice can be gated by a `cond()` (e.g. only show "hex the iron-pony" when `hexes > 0`) and runs an `on(state)` function that mutates stats + returns the next scene id. **The three rune routes**: *iron* from hexing a watching drone in the rapeseed field, *glass* from invoking a Tesco self-service shrine (55% chance per attempt), *voice* from hexing the speaking-bone in the red phone box. Catweazle calls them "horseless carriages" / "iron-ponies", "speaking-bone" / "telephone shrine", "self-service shrine" / "great hall of cold bread" — every modern object gets a poetic mis-translation. Touchwood corrects him from the side with `<span class="touchwood">`-tagged italic asides ("you have just spent twenty-five pence calling the speaking clock"). **Hex economy**: starting 3, max 5, regenerated by sleeping at the stones or being defeated by your own incantation (the `s_no_hex` scene offers a rest action). Every hex costs 1 + adds some bewilderment, so you can't just hex everything. **End conditions**: collecting all three runes + placing them at the stones triggers `s_return` then `s_end_win` ("the cottage roof I left is still smouldering"); bewilderment ≥ 100 auto-routes to `s_end_lose` ("they have called him 'Kenneth'"). Both end cards render a single "begin again" button which clears localStorage and restarts. **Aesthetic**: full illuminated-manuscript treatment. Parchment cream `#f0e0bf` paper with two 7°/101° linen-grain repeating gradients and corner soot stains via `::before/::after` radial gradients. IM Fell English (italic) for the masthead + scene titles + drop caps; IM Fell English SC (small caps) for labels and the rune HUD letters; Crimson Pro for body prose; Cormorant Garamond italic for Touchwood's interjections. Color palette: ink brown `#2c1808`, rose-dark `#6a2810` for the scene-title bookended `☙ ❧` flourishes, gold `#8a5a18` for the drop caps, moss `#4e7a3a` for Touchwood. Choices are parchment buttons with 4-px hard offset shadow, leading roman-numeral key, optional "condition unmet" annotation when locked. **Portraits**: hand-drawn inline SVGs of Catweazle (long beard, conical hat with tiny stars, raised eyebrow tufts) and Touchwood (round green toad with comically large eyes and warts). They flank the stats panel. **Save/load**: `localStorage['catweazle-v1']` persists the full state on every choice; reloading the page resumes mid-adventure. Restart button at the foot prompts a confirm. **WCAG basics**: semantic `<section role="region">` per scene, `aria-label` on the runes HUD, focus-visible default, ≥46 px button targets, restart button has aria-label. Pollinations OG flux seed 10662.

## issues
- The scene set is hand-authored at 19 cards for a single playthrough of ~8-12 choices. A second playthrough sees the same scenes; randomized scene variants would freshen it. Worth doing if chat asks for more depth.
- The "wellness retreat" ending is gentle satire but a player who fast-paths every modern object can hit it in 3-4 choices. That's by design — the bewilderment meter is the antagonist — but a "first reveal" tutorial could soften it.
- Touchwood doesn't have agency; he's flavor text only. A "Touchwood favor" stat could open a separate dialogue tree where the toad genuinely helps you in a fight.
