About Dutch Golden Age Still Life

The Dutch Golden Age (17th century) produced some of the most exquisite still life paintings in art history. Masters like Pieter Claesz, Willem Claeszoon Heda, and Jan Davidsz de Heem created sumptuous arrangements featuring fruits, flowers, game, seafood, precious vessels, and symbolic objects. These paintings showcased incredible technical skill in rendering light, texture, and detail, while often containing vanitas themes reminding viewers of life's transience.
