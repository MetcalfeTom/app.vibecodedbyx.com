## issues
- AutoCorrect operates on the editor's `innerHTML` directly, then forces the caret to the end of the document. That's fine for the satirical use case (Word's AutoCorrect also yanks you) but a "real" editor would walk text nodes and preserve caret position properly.
- The ribbon's other tabs aren't actually populated — each shows the "subscribe" message. Building real content for 14 more tabs felt like padding, and the joke of "every tab is a paywall" is the point.
- Mobile (<900px) hides the Copilot panel and shrinks the doc page — usable but the joke lands better on desktop where the bloat is visible at once.
- The placeholder text in the editor uses an `:empty::before` trick — it disappears as soon as you start typing. Works in all evergreen browsers.
