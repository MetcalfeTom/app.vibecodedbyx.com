# neon-city-bloxx

## log
- 2026-05-26: Initial build. Nokia Tower Bloxx / Stacker-style block-stacking game with neon synthwave skin and a toggleable optimization solver. **Core loop**: a block sweeps left↔right at the top of the tower, oscillating between the canvas walls; Space or tap drops it; the falling block accelerates under gravity (1600 u/s²) and lands on the previous floor. Overlap is sliced — any overhang on the left or right becomes a "scrap" piece that tumbles off-screen with its own physics (initial outward vx ±90–160, vy −40 to −80, vRot ±0.4–1.0 rad/s, gravity 1100 u/s²). Block width gets trimmed to the overlap region. Land entirely off the previous block → collapse. **Perfect drop**: when block centers align within 2.5px, block regenerates +6 px of width (capped at MAX_WIDTH 130), recentered around the previous block's center, with a 14-particle gold sparkle burst and ascending 880→1320→1760 Hz triad. Sliced more than 45% (overlap < 55% of prev width) flashes "SLICED" magenta and plays the high-Q noise + low thud. **Speed scaling**: base sweep 170 u/s + 9 u/s per floor, capped at 540 u/s. **Sway**: after floor 8, `swayMag` ramps to 0.05 over ~12 floors. Each stack block visually translates by `sin(swayPhase * 1.6 + floor * 0.06) * swayMag * 18 * swayInfluence`, where swayInfluence = `floor/totalFloors * 0.7 + 0.3` (top floors sway more). Sweep block also slightly drifts with the sway. Sway is mostly visual but does perturb the sweep's effective drop position (the wall bounds shift slightly), so it does subtly affect targeting at high floors. **Camera**: lerps toward keeping the topmost block at ~30% of canvas height. World content is drawn translated by `cam.y`. **Background**: 4-stop sky gradient (deep void → magenta-pink at base), moon with radial glow + sun-disk, 80 deterministic-positioned stars, 3 parallax neon city silhouette layers (cam-offset-scrolled, with hue-tinted top edge lines + tiny lit windows on each building generated from a deterministic seed). Ground glow at the bottom. **Block render**: vertical gradient (75% lightness → 52% → 32%) in the block's HSL hue, 2px inner highlight strip at top, 3px shadow strip at bottom, hue-glow stroke outline, plus 2-4 tiny window dots per block (count scales with width). 8-hue wheel cycles per floor (cyan/magenta/gold/violet/lime/red/blue/pink). **Optimization solver**: toggleable from the bottom HUD. Strategy: each frame, compute `sweepCenter` and `targetCenter = prevBlock.x + prevBlock.w/2 + Gaussian(σ=1.8 px)`. Predict next-frame sweep position via velocity. If the sweep will cross the target this frame OR is within 1.0 px, drop immediately. Box-Muller normal sampling for the noise term so the tower eventually drifts off — solver is *very* good but not infinite. While solver is on: a pulsing lime crosshair renders at the previous-block center to make its targeting visible, and the solver card appears in the top HUD. **Game over**: 700ms result modal pop-in with floor count + perfects + best (separate "best" tracking for solver vs human in localStorage `neon-city-bloxx-best` and `neon-city-bloxx-best-solver`). **Audio** (Web Audio synth): swoosh = bandpass-noise 2200Hz + triangle 660→280; thud = 90Hz sine + low bandpass noise; slice = high-Q 3400Hz noise + 1400Hz square chirp + low thud; perfect = 3-note ascending triangle triad (5ms stagger); gameover = 220→60Hz saw + heavy noise + 110→50Hz square thud. **Mobile**: 6rem gold DROP button bottom-right, tap-on-canvas also drops. **HUD top**: floor / best / solver pill (only shown when solver on). **WCAG**: rem units, semantic main/h1, role=application + descriptive aria-label on canvas, aria-live on prompt, aria-pressed on solver+mute, role=dialog aria-modal on result, focus-visible 3px outlines, ≥2.5rem touch targets, prefers-reduced-motion damps dt ×0.9. **JSDOM smoke test**: zero errors, solver toggle flips aria-pressed + reveals solver card, after 5s of solver autoplay floor=2 (rAF runs slowly in JSDOM so a real browser stacks ~10× faster). **Aesthetic**: Audiowide title with cyan→magenta→gold gradient fill, Major Mono Display tag, Share Tech Mono body, Rubik Mono One for HUD numerals. Lime accent reserved exclusively for solver UI to make its mode obvious.

## issues
- Solver Gaussian noise σ=1.8 px is tuned for "eventually loses" — too high and it dies in 20 floors, too low and it goes forever. May need rebalancing if the speed scaling changes.
- Sway physics are mostly visual; if I wanted solver to be challenged earlier, the previous-block center would need to physically shift each frame (currently only the visual translate moves). Acceptable for v1.
- Camera scroll uses world-coords + a single `ctx.translate(0, cam.y)` block; if I add multi-layer parallax I'll need to refactor.
