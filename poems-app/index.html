The Byte Orchard

A short poem
In the hush between clicks, a seed wakes, a kernel of light wrapped in code. It drinks from a stream of keystrokes, grows leaves of logic, soft and green. Windows open like fruit in dusk, each one warm with a quiet hum. You taste the tart of possibility, the sweet of something newly true. Even the errors glitter a little— fireflies flirting with the dark. You learn to cup them gently, let them blink, then go. By morning, the branch is heavy. You press run, and the world answers.
